Case Study #3

box

This project focuses on improving the usability of directional signage in a movie theater to reduce customer confusion and wait times for mobile order pickups. Through observational research and heuristic evaluation, I identified critical issues in wayfinding that negatively impacted the customer experience and proposed actionable design solutions.

    Problem

    Betrayed expectations

  • Misleading signage (white circle) consistently led guests to wait for their pick up inside the red circle
  • Mobile order is supposed to be quick and easy, however, the in-theater experience does not support that

    • Obstructed visual cues

  • Guest remain unaware that they are supposed to wait in the green circle due to the lack of visual distinction between two different pickup locations
  • Severity is directly correlated with foot traffic

    Design recommendations

    Move the point of entry and signage

  • Correct pickup location is immediately visible
  • Guests avoid misleading cues

    • Add subsequent signage

  • clearly label the hot-food pickup as such
  • Point out that the mobile-order pickup is elsewhere

    Impact

  • Reduce the cognitive load and enable guests to make critical decisions with confidence
  • Reduce ambiguity and align first impressions with the physical workflow
  • Time is no longer wasted and the reason for frustration disappears
