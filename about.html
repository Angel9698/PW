My Stance on AI

The world is evolving at a pace many of us cannot keep up with. Many groups have learned to incorporate AI into their daily workflows, while others turn to AI for more critical decisions. As a side effect, some companies favor AI work over grounded human labor for various reasons; whether it be cheaper, faster, or whatnot. However, we shoud never forget that while AI may try to seem human-like, AI will never be human. AI can simulate feelings of happiness or grief to tell a story, but it has never felt those feelings, so what would it know? AI can edit and generate images, transcribe documents and meetings, produce intital drafts, etc. AI is doing all sorts of things to help us in our day-to-day lives, but if you want people to connect with your work, to share and revel in the feelings you invoke through your work, that can only come from within, deep inside our hearts (don't worry, I cringed too).
Thus, I use AI as a junior assistant in my workflow, such as drafting a user persona, brainstorming ideas, or drafting questions to use in a usability testing session. Not only does this accelerate my work, but all the major and critical decisions always stay with me.
